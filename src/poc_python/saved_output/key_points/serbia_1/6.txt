1. The country has an emerging market economy in upper-middle income range.
2. Serbia is one of the largest providers of frozen fruit to the EU and a big producer of fruits like raspberries, blackberries, apples and plums.
3. Main industrial sectors include: automotive, mining, non-ferrous metals, food-processing, electronics, pharmaceuticals, clothes.
4. The energy sector is one of the largest and most important sectors to the country's economy.
5. Over 300 km (190 mi) of new motorways were constructed in the last decade and additional 154 km (96 mi) are currently under construction.
6. Rail services are operated by Srbija Voz (passenger transport) and Srbija Kargo (freight transport).
7. There are three airports with regular passenger services reaching over 6 million passengers in 2022 with Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport serving bulk of it.
8. Fixed telephone lines connect 81% of households in Serbia, and with about 9.1 million users the number of cellphones surpasses the total population by 28%.
9. Tourism is mainly focused on the mountains and spas of the country, which are mostly visited by domestic tourists, as well as Belgrade and Novi Sad.
10. The automotive industry is dominated by cluster located in Kragujevac and its vicinity, and contributes to export with about $2 billion.
